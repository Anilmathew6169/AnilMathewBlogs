How to set up a portfolio within 10 minutes for Free

March 2021


What is a portfolio?


A portfolio is a compilation of work samples and professional documentation that provides proof of your accomplishments or samples of your work. It can be a physical book or binder that organizes samples of your work, or an online portfolio with electronic files.


When and why do you use portfolios?


Portfolios are a great way to demonstrate the competencies you would list on a resume or talk about in an interview — they allow you to show and not just tell. During a job search, the portfolio showcases your work to potential employers. It presents evidence of your relevant skills and abilities. Portfolios are also helpful for independent contractors, consultants, or business owners who need to provide work samples to potential clients.

Outside of a job or client search, archiving samples of your work to a portfolio is a great way to keep track of your accomplishments and make note of when you acquired key competencies. Having it all together in a portfolio can be useful during your yearly review or helpful if you decide to go for a promotion. It can take quite a while to put together a portfolio, so make sure it’s up-to-date to prepare you for unexpected situations like layoffs and sudden changes to your job.


How do I create a portfolio?


The first thing you need to do is, create a template for your portfolio. You have to create it using HTML. If you are a computer science student you can create it by yourself. If your are not, and you don't want to code, then you can simply download a template online for free. There are tons of free templates are avilable online. Some of the links are given below:

-> https://bootstrapmade.com/bootstrap-portfolio-templates/
-> https://www.free-css.com/template-categories/portfolio
-> https://colorlib.com/wp/cat/portfolio/

Download a template from any of the websites and extract the downloaded file. Make sure that you have an 'index.html' file. This file will be your home page for the portfolio. Then applay the changes and modifications you preffer.

Ater your edits and modifications, your portfolio is now ready to host. We will use github to host the portfolio. Hosting in github is free and you only nee an account to start!


-> To host your portfolio, you need to install 'git' to your computer.
----> (If you already have it, then skip this step).

----> To download git: https://git-scm.com/downloads
----> After download open the executable file and follow the install instructions.
----> After finishing the installation follow this link to create your identity: https://git-scm.com/book/en/v2/Getting-Started-First-Time-Git-Setup

First, follow the link: https://github.com/
-> Sign in, if you already have an account or Sign up to create a new account.
-> After sign in, click on 'Start a project'.
-> Provide a repository name (Example: 'PortfolioSite').
-> Next, Make sure you are selected 'public'.
-> Next, Click on 'Create repository'.

Now, you will see 3 ways to upload your files to the repository.

Now, go to the folder location on your desktop where you have your portfolio code.
Now, right-click select option 'Git bash Here' option. After opening the git terminal,
-> Type 'git init' press enter key.
-> Type 'git add .' press enter key.
-> Type 'git commit -m "initial commit" .' press enter key.
-> Go to the github page and copy the remote origin link and paste it in the terminal. -> In my case it is: 'git remote add origin https://github.com/Anilmathew6169/AnilMathewBlogs.git'
-> Next, type 'git push -u origin main' press enter key.(You will be asked for your github username and password)
-> Now, your portfolio is pushed into the github. You can see the files in your project directory (click on the project name at the top of the github page)
-> Now, click on 'settings' top of the page.
-> Scroll down to 'Github pages'.
-> Change the 'Source' -> 'None' to 'Master branch'.
-> Click 'Save'.
-> Now, a new link will be generated at the 'Github pages' section. This will be the link to your portfolio.
-> open the link in new tab. You have your portfolio!
